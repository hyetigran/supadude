# Supa Dude is a finite, authored level — not an endless runner

Auto-run + dodge genre conventions (Chrome Dino, Canabalt) default to an endless, procedurally-generated run scored by distance, and we initially scoped it that way (fully procedural spawning, a distance+coins score, a leaderboard of best scores). Requiring three specific mini-boss encounters and a length comparable to "a long Geometry Dash level" forced a reconsideration: fixed, deliberate boss placement is incompatible with fully-random spawning, and Geometry Dash-style levels are finite, hand-paced, and end in either completion or a retry — not infinite survival. We chose a finite, hand-authored (or authored-chunk) level with a definite end over the endless model. This changes what "Score" and the Leaderboard mean (see follow-up decisions on completion metric and retry/checkpoint behavior) and means obstacle/power-up/boss placement is deliberate rather than randomly spawned.
